## The record

This term confirmed what Gorsuch-watchers have long known: he is the most method-bound Justice on the bench, and his method does not care which side wins. In a single week he wrote the 6-3 majority in *Landor v. Louisiana Department of Corrections*, telling a Rastafarian inmate whose head was forcibly shaved that RLUIPA's Spending Clause "contract" binds the State but not the guards who did the shaving — a clean, cold, contractual syllogism. Days later he concurred in *Chatrie v. United States*, voting with the Court's liberals that a Google geofence dragnet is a Fourth Amendment search, and grounding his vote in property and positive law rather than the majority's reasonable-expectations gloss. The same Justice who closed a remedy in *Landor* opened a privacy front in *Chatrie*. That is not inconsistency. That is a theory doing its work.

## Where he's pushing the law

Two engines drive Gorsuch. The first is a textualism so literal it routinely embarrasses the conservative coalition that confirmed him. He dissented alongside Justice Jackson in *Monsanto v. Durnell*, insisting FIFRA does not preempt a cancer-stricken plaintiff's failure-to-warn claim, and a year earlier he wrote the 7-2 majority in *Bondi v. VanDerStok* upholding the ATF's "ghost gun" rule over the howls of the gun-rights bar. Follow the words, he keeps saying, even when they wound your friends.

The second engine is structural, and here he is not cautious but evangelical. In *Trump v. Slaughter* he joined the majority burying *Humphrey's Executor* — then concurred to say the Court had not gone far enough. In *Trump v. Cook* he signed Alito's dissent, ready to let a President fire a Federal Reserve governor on a pretext. On the separation of powers, Gorsuch is the Court's accelerationist: the administrative state is a constitutional error to be unwound, and he wants the unwinding faster.

## My take

I admire the consistency and distrust the certainty. Gorsuch's refusal to let outcomes drive his reasoning is a genuine judicial virtue — it produces the cross-ideological surprises, *Chatrie* and *Monsanto* among them, that make him the most interesting vote on the Court. But the same purity curdles in the structural cases. A method that yields "no remedy" for a shaved-bald prisoner and "fire the Fed chair" for a vengeful President is a method that has stopped asking what its conclusions cost. Gorsuch writes as though law and consequence were unrelated. They are not.

His trajectory is unmistakable. He is the intellectual leader of the Court's anti-administrative project and the Justice most willing to say the quiet part — *go further* — out loud. That makes him essential reading, and increasingly the man drawing the map the others follow.
