## The record

Look at the last run of decisions and a pattern jumps out: Sonia Sotomayor loses the big cases and wins the long argument. In *Trump v. Slaughter* she lost — 6-3, the majority overruling *Humphrey's Executor* and handing presidents the power to fire the heads of "independent" agencies at will — but she read her dissent from the bench, a rare move reserved for moments she regards as a five-alarm fire. Her warning that "dozens of independent commissions are now likely to become purely executive agencies" is the line history will quote, not Roberts's tidy syllogism about Article II.

The losses pile up because the math is what it is. She wrote the principal dissent in *Mullin v. Al Otro Lado*, where a six-justice majority read the single phrase "arrives in the United States" to strand asylum seekers on the Mexican side of the line. She dissented in *Cisco Systems v. Doe* as the Court slammed the Alien Tort Statute shut on corporate complicity abroad, and she joined the dissents in *Mullin v. Doe* (TPS terminations made effectively unreviewable) and *Wolford v. Lopez* (Hawaii's carry rule struck down).

## Where she's pushing the law

But read her as a mere dissent machine and you miss the craft. When the Court splits her way, she is right there in the majority and unsentimental about it. She joined Kagan's opinion in *Chatrie v. United States* extending *Carpenter* to geofence warrants — a genuine Fourth Amendment win, and her oldest cause. She joined the Chief in *Trump v. Cook* to keep a Federal Reserve governor in her seat, and Barrett's majority in *Watson v. RNC* protecting mail-ballot grace periods in some thirty states.

Most telling is *Monsanto v. Durnell*, where she joined a 7-2 conservative majority on FIFRA preemption — against a Jackson-Gorsuch dissent. That is not a justice voting her feelings. That is a former prosecutor and trial judge who still reads the statute, even when the statute cuts against the injured plaintiff. Her method is fact-intensive and consequence-aware, and it does not bend to make the bloc tidy.

## My take

Sotomayor is the most consequential dissenter on this Court, and she knows exactly what she is doing. The bench dissents, the "with fear for our democracy" cadence, the refusal to launder bad outcomes into reasonable-sounding prose — this is deliberate institutional dissent, written for a majority that does not yet exist. The risk is that the role calcifies into prophecy nobody can act on, and that the heat occasionally outruns the analysis. But on a 6-3 Court tilting power toward the presidency, someone has to keep the receipts in plain language. She has appointed herself to that job, and no one does it better.
